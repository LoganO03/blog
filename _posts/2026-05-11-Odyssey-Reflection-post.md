#### SW Odyssey Reflection
For this project, my team and I worked with the Faulkner County Library's Seed Library as a community partner. In this project, we built a website for the library so that their inventory could be better managed, the public could view the inventory, and make it easier to be a part of the Seed Library's mission. We developed this site with the skills we developed in the classroom and through our coursework. 

The skills that become necessary for the success of this project are a knowledge of databases, a strong understanding of Razor Pages, teamwork, and communication. I feel that my abilities have grown further as a result of this project, as I feel databases are one of my technical strong suits at this point, and my ability to work and communicate in a team has improved from not just the work but the people around me.

When considering the goals and means the Library uses to benefit the community and the environment, I find them highly effective. By providing the resources and means for community members to learn about and participate in gardening, as well as making sure good seeds don't go to waste, they are creating real and lasting change. What I appreciate most about their approach is that it works through participation rather than pressure. They aren't telling anyone what to do; they're just making it easy and inviting to get involved, and that kind of change tends to stick.

This experience confirmed to me that technology is most valuable when it solves a real problem for real people. There is a real difference between building something for a grade and building something that will eventually cause a positive change in the community. That idea helped to change how I thought about every decision we made. Though we were only requested to build a simple database and web application, my group decided to go beyond their expectations, trying to deliver the best product we could in the time given. My coursework in databases and web systems gave me a strong foundation, and I found myself drawing on what I'd learned to help my team deliver a good product for our partner.

Before this project, my idea of doing meaningful work in tech was pretty abstract. This experience made it concrete. Seeing a community-rooted organization do genuine good shifted how I think about where I want to put my skills going forward. I'm more interested now in who technology is built for, who benefits from it, and how it can better communities, not individuals. I hope to find more opportunities like this so that I can continue serving my community and try to leave the world better than I found it.
